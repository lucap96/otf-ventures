The sourcing model
Three relationships. One compounding edge.
01
We started in venture with our entire friendship group
We were each at a big fund, so whenever a lead happened, we always knew first. This is how we got the {Ωperators} on board. Because we were already inside the deal flow, we could invite them into rounds alongside strong lead investors, derisking the bet through the quality of the other participants on the cap table. That gave them a reason to join, and gave us a track record of co-investment that compounded from there.
02
~50% of our deals come from other VCs — who are now also our LPs
We started becoming known as the operators who actually bring expertise. Operators are not a VC's bread and butter. They need us to fill the allocation they cannot. When a GP closes a round and wants operator presence on the cap table, they call us. They do not see us as a competitor. They see us as the piece they cannot build themselves. This is why so many of our co-investors have now also become LPs.
03
New ideas go to market participants first — not VCs
If someone has a new idea they go to operators, incumbents and market participants, not investors. Our {Ωperators} sit inside those loops. We hear of new ideas before they ever reach the VC world and then we send them to our VC network. This is why we have very strong GP relationships. We see it first, they get the deal. And we fit in perfectly: with record dry powder and a retracting growth stage, early-stage became a bidding war and the few people founders still welcome are exactly those who we have been working with for years.
The result
1,500+ deals in 2025. Not from a database. From relationships built over a decade.
