Exit Strategy

Realising returns is an
active, judgment-driven process.

Venture-backed IPOs and M&A exits in Europe are rare. We do not want to rely solely on those to achieve liquidity. As investors who have observed growth markets first-hand, we have seen how they periodically detach from fundamentals. Rather than being subject to that irrationality, we position to benefit from it — converting momentum into liquidity at moments when the market is most willing to pay.

01 — The Opportunity

Where the liquidity actually is.

€59B in European dry powder is chasing a shrinking pool of quality growth assets. The few teams who become the outliers will attract the majority of this capital — because there is nowhere else for it to go.

Why growth rounds are our primary exit window
Demand already exists
Quality assets attract oversubscribed growth rounds. We are not manufacturing a buyer — we are selling into one that is already there.
Capital has nowhere else to go
Independent of whether the long run is guaranteed, €59B in dry powder with no quality growth assets to absorb it will drive short-term markups into the best performing companies. We sell into that moment.
No process to run
We don't shop positions. We don't wait for an M&A window. Liquidity is a byproduct of company performance — not a separate problem to solve.
02 — The Art of Selling

A privilege, not a default option.

Most funds cannot sell into a round. We can — and the reason is structural.

Why others cannot do what we do
Large funds face signalling risk. If a tier-one fund sells, the market notices and draws conclusions. It is structurally impossible for them to exit quietly.
Small funds face a different problem. Why would the founder or the investors absorb the additional operational complexity of a secondary that does not benefit them? There is no incentive to facilitate it.
We sit in neither trap. Our {Ωperators} are the closest people to the founder at the moment it matters. In Lovable's case, Fredrik and Adam had such a close relationship with the founders that we could immediately move — rather than relying on a negotiated right.

Operator proximity to founders is a structural exit advantage — not just a sourcing one. The relationships that get us into the best deals are the same relationships that unlock liquidity when it matters.

03 — When We Sell

Our north star is realised cash.

When a position is significant for the fund, we sell. This does not mean a marginal uplift but a multi billion valuation that moves the fund. The math makes the case better than any thesis statement.

Why time destroys IRR — and why we sell early

IRR is brutally time-sensitive. The same 50% IRR requires 2x in 2 years — or 102x if you hold for 10. Waiting for the final multiple is almost always the wrong trade.

That doesn't mean we sell mechanically. We weigh how much upside is genuinely left, where the company sits in its trajectory, and what the market is willing to pay. But we do not let the pursuit of a larger paper multiple erode a real, already-exceptional return.

We focus on locking in top realised capital. Beyond that, we return the capital and make it the LP's choice whether they want more exposure to the underlying asset.

04 — Beyond the Fund

An exit is not the end — for those who don't want it to be.

Our primary obligation is to return and maximise the fund. Top-percentile realised returns, on the shortest credible timeline, is the only metric that matters at the fund level.

For LPs who want to maintain exposure to specific assets as they enter the growth stage, we facilitate continued participation through dedicated co-investment vehicles — with no fund costs. Participation is entirely optional and sized to individual preference.
