02 — The reality of finding an outlier

At pre-seed, nobody knows. Not even the best.

There is no quantifiable data, the product will pivot, the market will shift, the team evolves. Everything you think you know at pre-seed is provisional.

No revenue
Too early for any financial signal to mean anything.
Market unproven
The TAM thesis is a bet, not a fact.
Product will pivot
Most outliers change significantly post-investment.
Team is all you have
And even team assessment is deeply subjective.
2%
The base rate for backing a genuine outlier at pre-seed. One in fifty.

05 — Is this spray and pray?

No. Access is the filter.

Going wide only works if you are in the right deals. In today's market, a founder has no reason to take your money unless you bring something real.

The market problem
€59B in European VC dry powder. Everyone moved earlier. Pre-seed rounds are now bidding wars.
The founder question
There are 100 funds who want to back me. Why should I take yours?
Our answer
Elite {Ωperators} with $1.2B+ raised and 3,000+ hires. Founders invite us in. We are not competing for allocations. We are chosen for them.

We do not go wide into the general market. We go wide into the deals our {Ωperators} surface first. Access is the filter. Breadth is the strategy.

Diversification in venture is a misconception. Going wide is not about hedging or spreading risk; it is about maximising the probability that you capture the outlier. The power law drives return concentration regardless of portfolio size. Whether you start with 20 assets or 100, if the fund performs, one or two companies will drive the majority of returns. The strategy is designed to ensure we do not miss the company that makes the fund.
